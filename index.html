The Purple Platform Performance Gap

📊 E-Commerce Analytics · Multi-Platform Benchmarking

The Problem: A D2C beauty brand was selling on three major platforms — Amazon, Nykaa, and Purple — but had no unified view of which platforms actually drove profitability. Marketing spend was distributed equally across all three, but nobody knew if that was optimal.

What I Built: An automated ETL pipeline tracking 8 critical metrics across all three platforms: ROAS, CAC, Conversion Rate, AOV (Average Order Value), Sell-through %, Return Rate, Out-of-Stock Frequency (OTIF%), and Turnover. Real-time dashboards compiled 90-day rolling trends.

The Discovery: Purple dramatically outperformed both competitors. 2.4% conversion rate vs. Amazon's 1.4% (71% higher) and Nykaa's 1.8%. Purple also showed 51.9% ROAS vs. Amazon's 16.3%, with superior sell-through (51.9%) and lower return rates (0.6%). The data revealed: Purple was the brand's most efficient channel by 3x, yet it was receiving only 1/3 of marketing budget allocation.

Why It Matters: Metrics alone don't drive decisions—patterns do. The 90-day trend analysis showed Purple's outperformance wasn't a spike; it was consistent. The brand reallocated budget: 60% to Purple growth, 30% to Nykaa optimization, 10% to Amazon maintenance. Result: projected 45% increase in platform profitability within 6 months.
